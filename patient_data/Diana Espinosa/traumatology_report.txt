Trauma Report

58‑year‑old patient with no significant past medical history referred from primary care for low back pain radiating to the right lower limb for 6 months. No traumatic history. No motor deficit; ambulation limited by pain.

Lumbar spine MRI shows L5‑S1 disc herniation involving the right neural foramen.

Examination: Positive straight leg raise on the right side.

Patient was informed of medical/conservative treatment options and potential surgical intervention. The patient understood and consented to surgery. A pre‑anesthesia assessment was requested, and the patient was placed on the surgical waiting list. For pain management, gabapentin 100 mg q12h and paracetamol/tramadol 75/650 mg q12h were prescribed.

Follow‑up after intervention.
