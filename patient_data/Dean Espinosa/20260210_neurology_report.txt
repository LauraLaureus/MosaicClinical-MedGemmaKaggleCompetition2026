Medical history:

- Right L5‑S1 radiculopathy causing lumbar sciatica

No personal history of headache

Home treatment: Enalapril 10 mg daily, Tamsulosin 400 µg daily, Gabapentin 100 mg every 12 h, Tramadol/Acetaminophen 50 mg every 12 h.

Reason for consultation:
59‑year‑old patient referred from primary care with a 6‑month history of treatment‑resistant headache. The patient reports holocranial oppressive‑type pain predominantly in the afternoons, partially responsive to first‑line analgesics. Improves with rest; worsens markedly during stress periods. Current frequency is 12–14 days per month. No photophobia or phonophobia. During most intense pain episodes, nausea occurs; no vomiting. No trigemino‑autonomic symptoms. Never taken preventive headache treatment. Has been on acetaminophen/tramadol for lumbar radiculopathy for 3 months and reports increased headaches since then.

Examination:
No neck stiffness. No papilledema. No ophthalmoplegia. No visual field deficits. No facial asymmetry. No limb claudication on anti‑gravity maneuvers. No sensory or pain deficit. Normal gait.

Additional tests:
- Noncontrast cranial MRI: no abnormalities of craniocerebral anatomy.
- Recent blood work: mild dyslipidemia; otherwise unremarkable.

Clinical assessment:
Tension‑type headache with component of opioid overuse.

Therapeutic plan:
Gradually taper tramadol under supervision of primary care physician. Initiate amitriptyline 10 mg nightly for 4 months as preventive headache therapy. For severe attacks, take ibuprofen 600 mg. Follow‑up in 4 months.
