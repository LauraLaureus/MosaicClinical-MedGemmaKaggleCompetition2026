Primary Care

64‑year‑old patient with a personal history of hypertension, hypercholesterolemia, and type 2 diabetes mellitus presenting for information regarding tobacco cessation. Reports prior autonomous attempts with abstinence periods of 1–2 months but relapse due to stressors. Works as an elder caregiver; reports high stress levels and associates smoking with work breaks and social contact (both friends and spouse are heavy smokers, none have considered quitting). Family history includes myocardial infarction in father who died young from this cause; patient was told his death was related to tobacco use. Currently in the contemplation stage after attending internal medicine for uncontrolled hypertension, where she was informed that her risk of MI is very high.

PLAN:  
Advise patient to identify alternative rest activities that divert her from consumption. Additionally, she should discuss non‑smoker leisure alternatives with family and friends. Schedule follow‑up in 15 days; if she is in the action phase, initiate citiciclina.
